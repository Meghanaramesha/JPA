Hi everyone, Each week I share examples of the most interesting monetization strategies, UI experiments, and live-ops features I come across in mobile games.

This week brought a mix of creative offers, new UI patterns, and some clever reward mechanics.

Here‚Äôs a quick recap!

### Mob Control ‚Äì Back to School Offers

Mob Control leaned into seasonality with a **‚ÄúBack to School‚Äù** themed offer. Instead of just pushing a standard pack, they wrapped it around a moment players already relate to. It‚Äôs a reminder of how powerful timely hooks can be ‚Äî they create urgency and make the content feel fresh without changing the core offer too much.

A few weeks earlier, Mob Control did something similar with their **‚ÄúTour De Mob‚Äù** offer, clearly inspired by the **Tour de France**. Again, the content itself wasn‚Äôt radically different, but by tying it to a cultural event, the offer gained new relevance and energy.

These kinds of seasonal or event-based hooks are a simple but effective way to stand out in a crowded market ‚Äî proof that timing and context can be just as powerful as the offer itself.

### Fortress Merge ‚Äì Speed-Up Offers

Wave-based games thrive on tempo. Fortress Merge monetizes this by letting players buy speed boosts for combat. It‚Äôs a small tweak, but it hits right at player psychology: reducing downtime, getting faster rewards, and staying ‚Äúin the flow.‚Äù Simple, but a strong lever for monetization.

![Article content](https://media.licdn.com/dms/image/v2/D4D12AQF1tUNe4vtyjQ/article-inline_image-shrink_1000_1488/B4DZlDWozeGgAQ-/0/1757771632216?e=1760572800&v=beta&t=iVRKhQoxD4NLci1srYTFq97PWd0KtI8nKMQsrSMdPtA)

### Payment Loading Screens

A new pattern has been popping up in iOS games: instead of the standard App Store confirmation overlay, some games now display custom loading screens with characters or animations during the payment process. I spotted this in titles like Royal Match and Match Villains. It‚Äôs an interesting way to keep players immersed in the experience, even in the middle of a transaction.

![Article content](https://media.licdn.com/dms/image/v2/D4D12AQGbXgg-NvSdng/article-inline_image-shrink_1000_1488/B4DZlDWozZIgAQ-/0/1757771634488?e=1760572800&v=beta&t=cqHGSaGv36GdKdU3vU3E1uJPtu4y05-bTeaByM7yKVQ)

### Coin Master ‚Äì Follow Screen

Coin Master added a cross-promotion screen encouraging players to follow them on X and YouTube directly from the game. It‚Äôs a subtle but effective way to build social reach outside the app and keep players engaged with the brand across channels.

![Article content](https://media.licdn.com/dms/image/v2/D4D12AQFXaD3ikfif5g/article-inline_image-shrink_1000_1488/B4DZlDWozYJIAU-/0/1757771632211?e=1760572800&v=beta&t=LIelKNzJq3DkkOpe-P7stex1RDs_2gEmv8xgnggCZ9s)

### Archero 2 ‚Äì Pick Your 7-Day Reward

Archero 2 experimented with player choice in daily rewards. Instead of a fixed calendar, players get to choose their 7-day reward path. This small layer of agency makes the rewards feel more personal and encourages consistency ‚Äî a nice twist on a tried-and-true mechanic.

![Article content](https://media.licdn.com/dms/image/v2/D4D12AQH96CBUFInbaQ/article-inline_image-shrink_1000_1488/B4DZlDWozXIcAQ-/0/1757771632174?e=1760572800&v=beta&t=ayxg5787MAMFENKj_bAefdSxbmuhhCBr8fevQRqcUr4)
