A few years ago, flooding your game with interstitials and rewarded ads was the solid strategy to reach fast revenue. But things have changed. Rising user acquisition costs, stricter privacy rules, and player fatigue from longer end cards have pushed studios to prioritize long-term retention and deeper monetization. With IAP, the payoff might take longer, but the revenue is more stable, scalable, and less reliant on volatile seasonality from eCPMs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y8XQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F161c39a0-3081-45df-b359-cf72af7aa21e_1010x640.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y8XQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F161c39a0-3081-45df-b359-cf72af7aa21e_1010x640.png)

Despite the shift, ads still play a significant role, but not all formats contribute equally. **Interstitials account for roughly 50%** of all ad revenue in mobile games. **Rewarded video accounts for approximately 35%**, while banners comprise the remaining 15%. Itâ€™s a clear sign of where user attention and monetization potential lie. Interstitials still dominate because of their sheer volume, while rewarded ads are more user-friendly but less frequent. **Banners, once a staple, now at 5-second refresh rates barely move the needle**.

Another factor behind the shift is the industry's transition to bidding-only ad stacks. While bidding has brought transparency and simplified operations, itâ€™s also led to **lower overall eCPMs** for many developers. The days of lucrative fixed-price deals and inflated waterfall CPMs are all behind us. With everyone competing in the same real-time auction, margins have tightened, especially for studios that once relied on negotiated network deals to boost ad revenue. The result? Ad ARPDAUs have plateaued or declined, prompting more studios to turn to IAP for growth.

Everyone talks about moving from ads to IAP. However, very few manage to achieve this. It is not just a switch you flip. It requires rewriting how your game is designed, how your users are segmented, and how your teams think about monetization. The studios winning right now are not the ones abandoning ads. They are the ones who ads show in their games and segment users based on various factors. The new blueprint is more comprehensive, slower-paced, and more complex to replicate.

That said, eCPMs may be entering a quiet renaissance. With platforms like AppLovin leaning heavily into e-commerce demand, think Temu, Shein, and Amazon-backed campaigns, ad rates are starting to climb again in certain pockets of the USA. However, this will also increase UA costs for game studios in turn. AppLovin is expected to roll out its e-commerce product globally before the end of the year, which could shift the economics once again, making ads a more profitable revenue stream, not just a fallback. Time will tellâ€¦
