Mobile gaming runs on trends. Whatever converts gets copied across minigame onboardings and UA creatives. In February, Playtika released *Disney Solitaire,* and it spread like wildfire. Unbeknownst to Playtika, the launch massively boosted downloads across the entire Solitaire genre.

Now a new trend is emerging. Savvy developers are taking evergreen categories with consistently high organic and paid downloads, combining them, and capturing demand from both at once. It sounds a bit wishy-washy, but let me explain by telling you about *JigSolitare* by Gamincat.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wWDX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F59288a9e-c958-43d4-a1d3-1e7bbf4dfbd3_1566x598.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wWDX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F59288a9e-c958-43d4-a1d3-1e7bbf4dfbd3_1566x598.png)

Soft launched in May 2025, it has already racked up 30 million downloads in under two months and scaled to roughly 2.3 million DAU. Based on my estimates, that puts daily ad revenue at around $130 to $150k, driven almost entirely by between level interstitials.

The core gameplay is a polished, classic jigsaw puzzle where matching pieces smoothly snap together to form a complete image. Once a puzzle is solved, the player sees an interstitial and immediately moves on to the next level.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2Mlr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F82645754-23a6-4228-8d24-0a2c50f710de_945x2048.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2Mlr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F82645754-23a6-4228-8d24-0a2c50f710de_945x2048.jpeg)

Despite the name JigSolitaire, there is very little actual Solitaire gameplay. Aside from a brief Solitaire style animation at the start of each puzzle, the mechanics are pure jigsaw. Thereâ€™s no Solitaire mechanics involved, except for the solitaire animation at the start of every puzzle. Thatâ€™s the brilliance, summarized relatively easily. Itâ€™s a puzzle game that lies about also being a Solitare game.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7luY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4840238d-23e4-4892-b7ba-0c5c3af199cd_454x1000.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7luY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4840238d-23e4-4892-b7ba-0c5c3af199cd_454x1000.png)

Looking at their UA, all creatives lean either Solitaire style or puzzle style. That means they can tap into both genres at once, run a wider creative mix, and drive cheaper CPIs at scale. The trend is so strong that even Royal Match has started copying the approach, running puzzle style creatives for their match 3 game.

In other words, this is not about inventing new mechanics, it is about smart positioning. By blending evergreen genres at the UA level, games like JigSolitaire expand their addressable audience, unlock cheaper CPIs, and scale faster.
