Let me ask you, what is the single most significant predictor of a user clicking on an ad? You might think it is the creative itself. But it is not. In reality, it is often the end card, the final screen ad networks place on every video creative, that has the most significant impact on Click Through and Install Rates.

Subscribe

Time to go back to April 2022. A simpler time in the mobile ecosystem. Rewarded ads were 30 seconds long and were exited with one click. Interstitial ads were closable after 5 seconds. This is when IronSource began exploring the impact of adding a 3-click-to-exit to the end of rewarded and interstitial videos. I am not sure what the results were, but three weeks later, AppLovin followed and added the same clicks to exit. The creative end card arms race had begun.

Now, in mid-2025, the max duration of AppLovin Rewarded ads is 99 seconds with a 13-second end card that requires two clicks to exit.

These changes were not about improving the player experience. They were about improving network margins. By forcing players to spend more time on the end card, networks drive higher Click Through and Install Rates on their campaigns. The problem is that every extra click away from your game increases the risk that the player will get frustrated and never come back.

Longer end cards indeed increase the eCPM and the ad revenue; however, I have found that the increase in eCPM between the most aggressive and the least aggressive end cards is **never more than 8 percent**. More often than not, the loss in retention from the most aggressive end cards is not worth it.

What you need to remember is that when you start with a new ad network, you are always automatically placed on the most aggressive end card settings. It is vital that for a game or app, you set clear rules on what you accept as your creative lengths and end cards. If you have different settings on different networks, that means that some networks have an unfair advantage in your ad stack. This is also why it is sometimes challenging to get your network partners to change settings, as they do not want others to have an unfair advantage.

I **recommend** having a person regularly check on creative and end card lengths from all ad partners. Then, when reaching out to ad partners for end card questions, always make sure you use the word‚Äù

Here is a format that I recommend you use:

***Hi X,***

***We are scaling up the number of impressions shown to users, but we are struggling with retention because the end cards are too aggressive.***

***We are looking for the following formats:***

***Inter: 5-second closable***

***RV: 30-second ad with 5-second end card with a maximum of three clicks to exit***

***We have sent the same message to all our other partners to ensure parity!***

**Best,**

In a competitive ad stack, control over your creative lengths and end cards is not just a detail; it is a lever that can protect your retention and keep your monetization balanced. The networks will continually optimize for their margins. Make sure someone on your side is optimizing for yours.
