# 20 Costly Production Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

![](https://www.gamigion.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/20-Costly-Production-Mistakes-and-How-to-Avoid-Them.jpg)

###### 20 Costly Production Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

1. Skipping Pre Production  
Validate gameplay, market fit, and timelines first.  
  
2. Ignoring Player Feedback Early  
Start playtesting early and often.  
  
3. Overcomplicating the Core Loop  
Focus on a simple, addictive loop first.  
  
4. Underestimating Live Ops Complexity  
Plan your live ops roadmap before launch.  
  
5. Unrealistic Milestone Timelines  
Set realistic milestones with buffer time for iterations.  
  
6. Poor Communication Between Teams  
Use daily standups and shared dashboards to stay in sync.  
  
7. Ignoring Post Launch Metrics  
Monitor retention, DAU, ARPU, and LTV to guide updates.  
  
8. Over Monetizing Too Soon  
Build engagement first, then monetize thoughtfully.  
  
9. Overworking the Team  
Foster a positive work culture and promote balance.  
  
10. Skipping Market Research  
Study your genre, competitors, and player expectations.  
  
11. Neglecting Scalability  
Build scalable systems early and test them thoroughly.  
  
12. Lack of Clear Documentation  
Maintain up-to-date documentation accessible to all teams.  
  
13. Failing to Localize Properly  
Localize content, events, & monetization for each market.  
  
14. Disregarding Accessibility Features  
Include options like subtitles, controls, and difficulty levels.  
  
15. Crunch Time  
Focus on sustainable schedules and proper resource allocation.  
  
16. One Size Fits All Monetization  
Segment your players and offer tailored experiences.  
  
17. Mismanaging Outsourced Teams  
Set clear expectations, regular check-ins, and shared milestones.  
  
18. Not Testing Enough Platforms  
Perform rigorous QA on every target platform.  
  
19. Ignoring Community Building  
Engage with players on social media, forums, and through events.  
  
20. Neglecting Post Launch Support  
Commit to ongoing updates and bug fixes to retain players.
