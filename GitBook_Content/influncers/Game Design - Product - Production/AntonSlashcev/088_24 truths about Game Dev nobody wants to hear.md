# 24 truths about Game Dev nobody wants to hear.

###### 

1. Funding always has strings attached.  
2. Big game ≠ big success.  
3. Your first game will probably suck; accept it.  
4. Sometimes market research beats pure creativity.  
  
5. Players care about results, not your effort.  
6. Monetization isn’t evil, it’s essential.  
7. “Fun gameplay” is deceptively hard to master.  
8. Nostalgia often sells better than innovation.  
  
9. Pleasing everyone ensures mediocrity.  
10. Ideas are common; execution sets you apart.  
11. Crunch means bad planning, not passion.  
12. Calculated risks pay bills; passion rarely does.  
  
13. Player expectations are unrealistic but crucial.  
14. Communication, not tech, is the biggest obstacle.  
15. Your favorite feature is likely the first to cut.  
16. Stories succeed through restraint, not complexity.  
  
17. Harsh feedback is valuable; praise misleads.  
18. Not all feedback deserves action.  
19. Great mechanics often need simplification.  
20. Players prefer strategic imbalance over perfect balance.  
  
21. Marketing is storytelling, often surpassing the game itself.  
22. Dev tools will fail; adaptability matters most.  
23. Originality is overrated; familiar concepts executed well win.  
24. Bug-free games don’t exist; prioritize the critical ones.
